    Label Noise Injection

    Applied controlled randomness to labels to simulate real-world data ambiguity and build robustness against borderline cases.

    def add_noise_to_targets(df, target_col='target_hard', noise_frac=0.1):
      """Add label noise to target column"""
      idx = df.sample(frac=noise_frac).index
      df.loc[idx, target_col] = 1 - df.loc[idx, target_col]
      return df

    Quantile-Based Stress Scenarios

    Mild Stress (target_or)
    UNRATE ≥ 75th percentile OR Retail ≤ 25th percentile

    Flags periods of either high unemployment or low retail traffic relative to historical patterns. Creates early warning indicators of potential market stress.

    Severe Stress (target_hard)
    UNRATE ≥ 75th percentile AND Retail ≤ 25th percentile

    Highlights combined macro and consumer-activity shocks as red-alert conditions requiring immediate attention. Identifies highest-risk periods for CRE investors.

    Why Quantile-Based Approach?

    Using quantile thresholds allows our model to adapt to changing economic conditions without requiring manual recalibration.

    Optimal Threshold Selection

    Why 0.35?

    We compute Youden’s J (youden_j = tpr - fpr) for each threshold, then pick the first threshold from the roc_curve output where True Positive Rate ≥ 0.80 and False Positive Rate ≤ 0.20, matching our portfolio risk guidelines.

    Business takeaway: This threshold delivers early warning signals while keeping false alarms within acceptable limits.
